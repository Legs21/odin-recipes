Cast Iron Steak

Description

Steak is a classic meal, and contrary to popular belief, can be made just as well at home with the right ingredients and some practice. The theory and steps are actually very simple, and over time, developing a feel for how long to cook each side will come naturally. The real key to this recipe is having a solid cast iron skillet that can handle high heat and put a good sear on your steak without ruining it.

Ingredients

  • Steak
  • Seasoning - Kosher salt and pepper
  • Vegetable oil
  • Butter
  • Rosemary
  • Thyme
  • Garlic

Steps

  1. Prep your steak by patting the sides with a paper towel. After that, sprinkle a good dose of Kosher salt on each side. Loosely wrap the steak in paper towels and set aside as you prep the pan. This process will hel draw the liquid out of the steak and assist you in making a nice sear.
  2. Turn your burner to medium high heat and place your cast iron skillet on top. A hot pan is crucial to a good sear.
  3. Pour some vegetable oil in the pan, then unwrap your steak and place it in the pan, flip after about 8 min depending on the size and desired finish.
  4. Finish this steak by spooning a base. in the pan, place a couple tsp of butter, 2 cloves of mashed garlic, rosemary, and thyme. Spoon the liquid over top of the steak for about a minute each side.
  5. Take the steak out and LET IT REST for 5-10 min. This is crucial to allowing th juices to set inside the steak. During the rest period the steak is still cooking. Use this time to prep your plate with any other sides you may have made.
